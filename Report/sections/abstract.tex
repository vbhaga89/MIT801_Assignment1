\begin{abstract}
Predicting 30-day hospital readmissions is essential for managing healthcare resources and providing necessary care to patients. Effective models must balance false negatives, which risk 
missed care, and false positives, which results in overwhelmed hospital staff. This study uses a logistic regression model trained with Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) on the 
UCI Diabetes 130-US hospitals dataset (1999–2008) to predict 30-day readmissions. The impact of hyperparameter tuning is evaluated to improve performance beyond baseline 
results. The results highlight the importance of model tuning in achieving a balance between precision and recall in the medical domain.
\end{abstract}
